Care Plan Pre-Assessment

Step 1: General Information

e.g., Mr, Mrs, Ms, Dr Try using the resident’s preferred name if they have one. e.g., “Johnny” or “Liz” — use correct pronouns (he/him, she/her, they/them). Auto-generated or manually entered. Signpost location if needed.

Step 2: Biography & Quick Notes

Provide a brief account of the resident’s life: past, present, interests, family, relationships, career, religious beliefs. Do not include medical information. Write facts only; avoid assumptions or personal opinions. Short immediate observations or personal preferences (non-medical if possible).

Step 3: Circle of Care

e.g., Daughter, Son, Best Friend

Step 4: Onboarding & Needs Assessment

e.g., Hospital, Home, Another Care Facility e.g., NHS, Private, Local Authority

Step 5: Clinical Information

Avoid “none” unless truly applicable. E.g., Allergic to penicillin, lactose intolerance. E.g., Vegetarian, Halal, Low-Salt. If bed-bound, note that they should sit upright at 90° for feeding. Who makes medical decisions in emergencies? How to notify next of kin? List diagnoses, relevant treatments, e.g. Diabetes, Epilepsy, Parkinson’s. Note any additional care plans needed. Type of medication (e.g., for Parkinson’s), side effects, food/drink to avoid. E.g. “Resident prefers to take meds crushed with applesauce. See Camascope for full details.”

Malnutrition Universal Screening Tool (MUST)

Auto-calculate or manually enter. E.g., 23.0 Evaluate risk: Low, Medium, High

Step 6: Mandatory Care Plan Sections

DNACPR or ReSPECT forms? Wishes for final days (music, visitors)? Funeral arrangements? Any POA or LPA? Cultural/religious preferences? Dementia, memory issues, how they prefer to communicate, support strategies. Hearing or speech impairments, communication aids, signposting to hearing aids, if relevant. Type/size of products, assistance required, prefer “support with toilet needs” over “toileting.” Identify mental health conditions, coping mechanisms, risk factors if untreated. Clothing, makeup, jewellery, chosen name/pronoun, hairdresser visits, etc. Hobbies, interests, social preferences, e.g., reading, music, outings. Summarise how staff/resident handle medication. If time-sensitive, detail instructions. E.g. “Staff to check for side effects, see Camascope for further info.” Note if resident is left-handed, can do buttons/zips, uses specialist equipment, history of falls. Signpost to manual handling instructions if needed. Food preferences, risk of choking, does the resident need a clothes protector? If bed-bound, ensure 90° angle while eating/drinking for 30 mins. Own teeth? Dentures? Electric/manual toothbrush? Where dentures stored at night? Mouthwash, floss, tepe brushes? Support needed for hair care, washing, trimming nails, hair removal, etc. E.g., “Resident chooses clothes, staff assist with shampooing her hair weekly.” Chronic conditions, specialist therapies, support from health professionals. Link to relevant care plans. Wounds, bruises, dryness, air mattress usage, involvement of DN/TVN or diabetic nurse. Night routine, removing dentures, light on/off, call bell within reach, how many nightly checks.

Step 7: Specialist Care Plans (If Applicable)

For any diagnosed condition that requires a separate or detailed plan. Examples to ask about:
  • Diabetes (blood sugar monitoring, insulin regimen, foot care)
  • Epilepsy (seizure triggers, rescue meds)
  • Parkinson’s (mobility changes, timing of medication)
  • ME/Chronic Fatigue (energy conservation, pacing)
  • Heart Disease (angina, heart failure, blood pressure management)
  • Anti-Coagulant (Warfarin, bleeding risk, INR checks)
  • COPD/Asthma (breathing exercises, inhalers)
  • Cancer Care (chemotherapy, pain management)
  • Behaviours that Challenge (aggression, triggers, self-harm)
  • Smoking or Alcohol/Drug Use

Step 8: Foot Care Assessment

Do they see a chiropodist every 6 weeks? Do staff assist with moisturizing?
