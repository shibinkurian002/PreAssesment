Care Plan Pre-Assessment

Step 1: General Information

e.g., Mr, Mrs, Ms, Dr Try using the resident’s preferred name if they have one. e.g., “Johnny” or “Liz” — use correct pronouns (he/him, she/her, they/them). Auto-generated or manually entered. Signpost location if needed.

Step 2: Biography & Quick Notes

Provide a brief account of the resident’s life: past, present, interests, family, relationships, career, religious beliefs. Do not include medical information. Write facts only; avoid assumptions or personal opinions. Short immediate observations or personal preferences (non-medical if possible).

Step 3: Circle of Care

e.g., Daughter, Son, Best Friend

Step 4: Onboarding & Needs Assessment

e.g., Hospital, Home, Another Care Facility e.g., NHS, Private, Local Authority

Step 5: Clinical Information

Avoid “none” unless truly applicable. E.g., Allergic to penicillin, lactose intolerance. e.g., Vegetarian, Halal, Low-Salt. If bed-bound, note that they should sit upright at 90° for feeding. Who makes medical decisions in emergencies? How to notify next of kin? e.g. Diabetes, Epilepsy, Parkinson’s. Note any relevant additional care plans needed. Type of medication (e.g., for Parkinson’s), side effects, food/drink to avoid. e.g. “Prefers meds crushed with applesauce. See Camascope for details.”

Malnutrition Universal Screening Tool (MUST)

Evaluate risk: Low, Medium, High

Step 6: Mandatory Care Plan Sections

DNACPR? ReSPECT forms? Funeral wishes, POA, cultural/religious preferences? Dementia, memory issues, communication strategies, etc. Hearing/speech impairments, communication aids, signposting to hearing aids, braille, etc. Type/size of continence products, help needed, routines. Clothing, makeup, jewellery, gender expression, hairdresser visits, etc. Hobbies, interests, outings, social preferences. Time-sensitive meds, side effects, signpost to Camascope or MAR charts as needed. Left/right-handed, assistive equipment, history of falls, signpost to manual handling plan. Food preferences, risk of choking, if bed-bound, must be at 90° for 30 mins after feeding, etc. Dentures? Electric/manual toothbrush? Mouthwash, floss, tepe brushes, etc. Support needed for washing, shaving, hair removal, nail care, etc. Chronic conditions, therapies, professional support, signpost to relevant care plans. Wounds, dryness, air mattress usage, involvement of DN/TVN, creams/emollients. Night routine, removing dentures, lights on/off, call bell, number of nightly checks.

Step 7: Specialist Care Plans (If Applicable)

For any condition requiring extra detail:
  • Diabetes, Epilepsy, Parkinson’s, Heart Disease, ME/CFS
  • Anti-Coagulant (Warfarin, INR checks), COPD/Asthma
  • Cancer Care, Behaviours that Challenge
  • Smoking, Alcohol/Drug Use, etc.

Step 8: Foot Care Assessment

Does a chiropodist visit every 6 weeks? Does staff assist with moisturizing?
